Restaurants

The Lucky Cat is an Asian-inspired cuisine hosted by no other than Gordon Ramsey himself. This open-kitchen restaurant crafts Michelin-star meals that are made with creativity and passion. This restaurant is situated next to the Manchester Art Gallery and pertains to a professional dress-code which is best suited for couples who want to de-stress and enjoy a relaxing evening. With a plethora of choices from the wine selection, the restaurant also serves cocktails with Japanese infused ingredients if you are willing to prestige your pallette. They offer vegetarian/vegan options upon being seated and it is in your best interest to notify chefs if you have any allergies!

Located In the Trafford Centre, Coast-To-Coast is an American-inspired restaurant that serves mouth-watering food that is sure to leave you wanting to come back! it's dim lit lighting and leather booth style seats make this a go-to for nights out with family members. The menu has a selection of American-style burgers, racks of ribs and a selection of delicious milkshakes like brownies & ice-cream that are guaranteed to satisfy your sweet tooth.

Pubs/Bars

The Alchemist is a bar (and restaurant)that focuses on the sheer craftsmanship of their cocktails. The dark, Victorian style setting sets the tone for the establishment as you make yourself comfortable. On Fridays, the bar has a professional DJ that plays a mix of soulful and disco house music. The bar has a range of gift sets, from bottled cocktail blends to alchemist gift cards which are a nice parting gift to that special someone that is always there for you.

Located in the city centre, The Old Wellington Pub is housed in one of the oldest buildings in Manchester! built in 1552, This Victorian era house is the perfect social place to meet new people and watch sports whilst indulging in their wide range of ales and whisky collection. The pub serves up piping hot pub classics to passer-by's in need of a quick energy boost or a good day out with families that are in the general area.
